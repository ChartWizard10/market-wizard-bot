## 2. Phase 14C Deployment Status — CONFIRMED NOT DEPLOYED

This is the primary finding of the Phase 14E audit. Both P1 (tight=-1) and P3 (UPGRADING +2→+1) are confirmed absent from the running bot as of the May 20 session.

### 2.1 Evidence from IESC — Elite Cap Fires (P1 Not Active)

The IESC calibration display reads:
> `89 calibrated (+1) — high-quality executable, near-elite but cleanliness preconditions not met.`

The phrase "near-elite but cleanliness preconditions not met" is generated by `_primary_reason()` **only when `elite_cap_applied = True`**. The elite cap fires exclusively when the pre-cap calibrated score reaches ≥ 90.

**Pre-P1 reconstruction (tight = 0):**
- Risk healthy/tight → 0 (tight not in `_RISK_ADJ` pre-P1, defaults to 0)
- Overhead clear (SNIPE_IT): +1
- Structure BOS: +1
- Trajectory REPEATED: 0
- Raw delta: **+2** → 88 + 2 = **90**
- 90 ≥ 90, `_qualifies_for_elite()` returns False (risk_state = tight → fails health check)
- Elite cap applied: calibrated → 89. `elite_cap_applied = True`.
- Display: `89 calibrated (+1) — high-quality executable, near-elite but cleanliness preconditions not met.` ✓

**Post-P1 reconstruction (tight = −1):**
- Tight: −1; clear: +1; BOS: +1; REPEATED: 0
- Raw delta: **+1** → 88 + 1 = **89**
- 89 < 90 → elite cap NOT fired. `elite_cap_applied = False`.
- Display: `89 calibrated (+1) — high-quality executable, {overhead or structure reason}.`

The "near-elite but cleanliness preconditions not met" text **cannot appear in the post-P1 code path** for this configuration. Its presence is definitive proof that P1 is not running.

## 5. R:R Inflation Analysis

### 5.1 Inflation Pattern — Three Sessions

| Session | Ticker | R:R | Stop % | Capital | Inflation Driver |
|---------|--------|-----|--------|---------|-----------------|
| May 15 | MOD | 7.91 | 0.58% | Full | Microscopic stop |
| May 18 | AFRM | 17.61 | 0.44% | Reduced | Microscopic stop |
| May 20 | ADI | 12.05 | 0.37% | **Full** | Microscopic stop |
| May 20 | IESC | 7.98 | 0.95% | Full | Compressed OB anchor |

R:R values of 12.05 and 7.98 at sub-1% stops are geometric artifacts. A 0.37% stop combined with any reasonable target produces a double-digit R:R by construction. The calibration layer does not currently distinguish legitimate high R:R (large target, reasonable stop) from inflated R:R (normal target, compressed stop).

**Implication for P2:** P2 (R:R quality adjustment) is the only mechanism that addresses this. Without P2, an ADI-style setup with 0.37% stop and R:R 12.05 receives identical calibration treatment to a 3.0% stop setup with R:R 4.0 — both could score the same delta. P2 is not yet approved for implementation, but the May 20 session continues to strengthen the case.
